Design

The story here is actually pretty underwhelming - this egg cup is actually the result of me messing about with 3D modeling. I wanted to design something simple - clean features and smooth transitions. And I'd say I succeeded.

What's more interesting is the reason why I designed this egg cup in the first place. In reality, creating the Stone grinder Medium was the main point, but there was a problem - I had no experiance working with the Type IV plaster, and I needed to learn somehow.

And since the best way to learn is by doing, I decided to do that. I thought that starting with something simple was the way to go, so i thought why not make something useful. And thus, this egg cup was created.

Unique mateiral

Type IV dental plaster

This type of plaster is normally used by dentists as a step in creating dental prostheses. But, as it turns out, it has may other uses - for example, hobbyists are using it to create realistic miniature stone wall replicas.

I decided to use this material for two main reasons: high strength and nice surface finish.

The strength of the material is really important, because you wouldn't want your egg cup to break. The Type IV plaster is known for its hardness as well as strength, making the grinder durable enough for everyday use.

The feel of the material, on the other hand, is just as important.

Stone grinder actually does feel like a stone. It has a smooth, matte finish, and is fairly heavy for its size - it feels similar to the touch as a stone you'd find by the river.
